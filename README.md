# Crime Analysis Dashboard - PowerBI
 
## Project Overview:
The Crime Analysis Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of crime patterns, trends, and key metrics to support data-driven decision-making for law enforcement and public safety.

### 🎯Goals:

#### 1. Monitor Overall Crime Statistics
Provide a consolidated view of total crimes, areas, districts, locations, and arrest statistics to assess overall crime activity.
#### 2. Analyze Crime Trends Over Time
Track crime occurrences by year, quarter, month, and day to identify seasonal trends and changes in crime patterns.
#### 3. Identify Crime Hotspots
Visualize area-wise and location-wise crime distribution to help law enforcement prioritize high-crime regions.
#### 4. Understand Victim and Offender Demographics
Analyze victim age groups, gender distribution, and offender status (adult/juvenile) to support targeted crime prevention strategies.
#### 5. Support Resource Allocation and Public Safety
Enable data-driven policing by identifying crime severity, weapon usage, investigation status, and arrest trends for informed operational planning.

### 📈Results / Insights:
#### 1. Crime Volume Exceeds One Million Incidents
The dataset contains over 1 million crime records (1.003M) across 21 areas and 66.5K locations, indicating extensive crime coverage.
#### 2. Crime Increased Significantly After 2021
Crime cases rose from approximately 126K in 2021 to 235K in 2022, remaining above 200K in subsequent years.
#### 3. Central Area Reports the Highest Crime Count
Central, 77th Street, and Southwest are among the areas with the highest number of reported crimes, making them major crime hotspots.
#### 4. Young Adults Are the Most Affected Victims
Young-age victims account for approximately 680K cases, significantly higher than middle-aged (290K) and older victims (30K). Male victims also outnumber female victims.
#### 5. Most Cases Remain Under Investigation
Around 803K incidents are classified as "Investigation Continued", while adult arrests (~86K) far exceed juvenile arrests (~3K), indicating adult offenders constitute the majority of arrests.
